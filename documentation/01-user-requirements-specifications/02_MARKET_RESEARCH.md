# Market Research

## 1. Industry Overview

Bangladesh's professional education sector has undergone substantial growth over the past decade, driven by a rising middle class, expanding corporate sector and increased demand for internationally recognised finance and accounting qualifications. ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants) is widely regarded as the most prestigious professional accounting credential accessible to Bangladeshi students, with pathways into multinational corporations, Big Four accounting firms and the financial services industry.

The market for ACCA tuition in Bangladesh is served by a mix of Approved Learning Partners (ALPs), independent tutors, and informal study groups. HeadStart occupies the ALP-accredited tier — a designation that signals quality and is granted directly by ACCA Global, giving accredited institutions a verifiable competitive advantage.

The broader EdTech market in Bangladesh is growing, with increasing smartphone penetration (currently exceeding 40% of the population) and improving internet infrastructure making digital learning platforms viable at scale for the first time.

## 3. Market Size & Opportunity

- ACCA Bangladesh currently reports over **14,000+ registered students** nationally, with consistent year-on-year growth.

- The Dhaka metropolitan area accounts for the largest concentration of ACCA students and approved learning partners.

- Skill Development Trainings (MS Office, AI, Tax & VAT) target an additional, broader audience of early-career professionals — a market segment largely underserved by traditional ACCA institutions.

- The shift toward blended and online learning post-2020 has created demand for institutions offering structured digital platforms, which most Bangladeshi ALPs currently lack.

---

## 4. Key Market Trends

- **Digital-First Expectations** : Students increasingly expect self-service enrolment, digital resources and on-demand learning — traditional in-person-only offerings are no longer sufficient.

- **Mobile Learning** : The majority of Bangladeshi students access educational content via smartphone. Any platform must be mobile-responsive from day one.

- **AI-Assisted Learning** : AI tutoring, query resolution and personalised study recommendations are emerging differentiators in the regional EdTech market.

- **Payment Localisation** : SSLCommerz and bKash dominate digital payments in Bangladesh. Platforms that rely solely on international payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal) face adoption friction.

- **Trust Signals Matter** : National prize winners, accreditation badges, alumni outcomes and pass rate data are primary conversion drivers for prospective ACCA students in Bangladesh.

- **Employer Partnerships** : Institutions that demonstrate active job placement and industry connections attract students who view ACCA as a vocational, not purely academic, qualification.

## 5. Regulatory & Accreditation Context

- HeadStart operates as an **ACCA Approved Learning Partner (ALP)** — a status governed by ACCA Global's quality frameworks and subject to periodic review.

- The LMS and course content must remain aligned with ACCA's published syllabi and examination schedules.

- Student data handling must comply with applicable Bangladeshi data protection norms and ACCA's institutional data governance expectations.

- Financial reporting (Phase 3) must align with Bangladeshi accounting standards (BAS/BFRS) for internal institutional use.
